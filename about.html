Get to know me

About Me

I never planned to work in cybersecurity. I wanted to be a vet, but my dad pushed me towards tech, and for a while, I resisted — until something clicked. About a year into my degree, I found myself drawn to the challenge of breaking down systems, solving problems under pressure, and spotting what others miss. What started as frustration quickly turned into something I genuinely enjoy, and now I can't imagine doing anything else.

At 18, I moved from Bahrain to Sheffield alone, and since then I've delivered eight cybersecurity projects and conducted original research, running 36 experiments on AI-assisted penetration testing. Alongside that, I competed in the England Handball Premier League, earning two MVP awards.

Before university, I spent six years balancing school, competitive sport, and working in a restaurant while graduating with a 3.77 GPA, and I also came back from an ACL injury that required surgery — an experience that built discipline and resilience early on. I learn fast, perform under pressure, and I'm ready to take on my next challenge anywhere in the world.

My View on Worldwide Theories

01
The Big Bang Theory

The universe began approximately 13.8 billion years ago from an infinitely dense singularity. What fascinates me is that a Belgian priest, Georges Lemaître, first proposed it — proof that science and faith are not always opposites.

02
Darwin's Theory of Evolution

All life on earth shares a common ancestor and evolved through natural selection. The species that survive are not the strongest — they are the most adaptable. I think about this beyond biology — it applies to people, businesses, and ideas too.

Manual of Me

Call or text — I respond best to direct communication. No need to overthink it, just reach out.

Best time to reach me

Anytime — I don't have a strict schedule for communication. If I'm busy I'll get back to you.

How I give feedback

Variable — depends on the situation. I adapt my approach based on what's needed in the moment.

What drains me

Unclear goals. When there's no direction or purpose, it's hard for me to stay motivated and focused.

What energises me

Stress and pressure. I perform best when the stakes are high — pressure brings out the best in me.
